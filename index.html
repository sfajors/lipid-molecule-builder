Introduction to Lipids

Introduction to Lipids

Lipids are a special kind of molecule that your body needs to stay healthy. But what exactly are they? Today, we're going to explore what lipids are, how they are named, and why they are so important. By the end of this lesson, you'll know all about the different types of lipids and their superpowers!

Importance of Lipids

Here are some cool things lipids do for you:
  • Store energy for when you need it.
  • Protect your organs by acting like cushions.
  • Help build cell membranes, which protect your cells.
  • Keep you warm, like a blanket.
  • Help certain vitamins work properly.

Lipid Nomenclature

Lipids have special names, just like superheroes! Let's learn about them:
  • Fats and Oils: Solid lipids are called fats, like butter. Liquid lipids are oils, like olive oil.
  • Fatty Acids: The building blocks of lipids. Some are straight (saturated), and some are bent (unsaturated).
  • Glycerol: A small molecule that acts as the backbone for triglycerides. It connects to three fatty acids to form a triglyceride.
  • Phospholipids: Lipids that help build cell membranes, with a part that likes water and a part that doesn't.
  • Sterols: Ring-shaped lipids like cholesterol that help build cells and make hormones.
